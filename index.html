Case Study: Enhancing Operational Efficiency at Northvale NHS Trust with AI Agent "Clara"

Overview

In a drive to improve operational efficiency and reduce administrative burden on clinical and non-clinical staff, Northvale NHS Trust implemented a digital AI agent named Clara. Designed as a virtual assistant powered by natural language processing and machine learning, Clara was deployed to support a range of tasks across outpatient services and administrative operations.

The Challenge

Northvale NHS Trust faced increasing pressure to manage rising patient demand with limited resources. Staff were spending a significant amount of time on repetitive administrative tasks such as appointment scheduling, patient follow-up reminders, and triaging non-urgent queries. This was not only inefficient but also impacted the Trust’s ability to meet key performance targets and provide a seamless patient experience.

AI Agent: Clara

Clara is a conversational AI agent developed in collaboration with a UK-based healthtech firm, tailored specifically for the NHS context. Built using secure and interoperable technology, Clara was designed to integrate with existing systems such as the Electronic Patient Record (EPR), appointment management software, and internal communications platforms.

Implementation

The Implementation of Clara Followed a Phased Approach:

  1. Discovery and Co-Design Workshops The Trust ran workshops with frontline staff and digital teams to identify priority use cases. This participatory design ensured Clara was configured to reflect real-world workflows and needs.
  2. Pilot Deployment in Outpatient Services Clara was first introduced in the outpatient department to manage appointment scheduling and send automated reminders. She could reschedule appointments based on patient input via SMS or a secure web portal, reducing DNA (Did Not Attend) rates by 22% within the first three months.
  3. Integration with Internal Communications Clara was then integrated with Microsoft Teams to act as a staff assistant. Team members could ask Clara to summarise meeting notes, locate internal documents, or flag urgent requests, reducing time spent on routine information retrieval.
  4. Expansion to Patient Triage The next phase involved Clara supporting non-urgent triage for common patient enquiries. Using a clinically approved decision tree, Clara could direct patients to self-care resources, book nurse consultations, or escalate to a clinician when necessary.
Outcomes
  • Time Savings: Staff saved an average of 4.5 hours per week, freeing up time for more patient-facing activities.
  • Improved Patient Experience: Patients reported quicker responses to routine queries and appreciated the ability to manage appointments at their convenience.
  • Operational Efficiency: A 35% reduction in administrative workload in pilot departments was recorded within six months of implementation.
  • Scalability: The success of Clara prompted plans for expansion into urgent care and community health settings.
Conclusion

The introduction of Clara at Northvale NHS Trust exemplifies how AI agents can support NHS priorities by improving efficiency, reducing staff burden, and enhancing patient engagement. Through careful planning, co-design, and phased implementation, Clara has become a trusted digital colleague—freeing up human time for where it matters most: patient care.
